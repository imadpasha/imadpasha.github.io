Creating Broad Composite SED's of High Redshift Galaxies

When looking at very faint distant galaxies, it is almost impossible to get high quality spectroscopic data. Usually, only photometric data can be obtained, however, this gives only a single point on a spectrum. Gravitational lensing is a powerful tool for probing high redshift galaxies at and beyond z=2, when the universe's star formation rate peaked.
