About Me


I was born in Brooklyn and spent my childhood in Mississippi, but thankfully at the age of five I ended up moving to California. I went to middle and high school in San Diego, and I am now a third year student at UC Berkeley (Go Bears). I am majoring in Astrophysics (because space is awesome) and Physics, and I am pursuing a minor in Creative Writing. The minor is both a balancing pressure valve from physics, and a creative outlet. My roommate and close friend is a Film major, and I am interested in film theory and production as a hobby. I also feel that writing is a valuable skill to hone, even/especially as a scientist.

My interests, in increasing orders away from Astrophysics, are astrophotography, teaching, filmmaking, writing and playing music, writing prose and poetry, watching Kubrick films with my roommate, html and web design (re: this site), rock climbing, hiking, and good food. I really enjoy teaching, and currently teach an introductory python class at Berkeley, as well as serving as a GSI (TA) for the introductory astronomy class taught by Alex Filippenko (more on that in the teaching subsection).
